Brief

Unlike most of other UX projects faced before; Fakulty is born from a problem statement ideated from scratch. Then, it needed to be validated by Ironhack’s Staff.

There were up to 6 proposals on the table before we all agreed to dig deeper into the idea of empowering any sort of community. At this point, the idea seemed to be exciting. However, there were still too many points to be precised.

The Challenge

As the idea was still abstract and unspecific. Creating something that worked for almost everybody, was something hard to tackle; It needed definition.

The priority was to come up with a sharper problem statement. Something certain that served in a particular way and that affected a precise group of people or community.

Students, universities... Overall, education seemed to be the right decision. Students have been and always be the future of any society.

Problem Statement

"The student's community worldwide needs voice, coordination and organization in order to take action and solve anything that affects their concerns."

Research

Both surveys and interviews were essential when it came to start developing this product. It was discovered that there's an actual problem among the students community. They feel frustrated and uncapable of facing certain situations because there's a lack of coordination or dependency of certain people or institutions.

Most of students think delegations barely work. There's a real frustration when it comes to propose anything to their universities.

93%

Go to delegates everytime they are worried about something.

87%

Think that student's delegations don't work properly.

71%

Claim not beeing updated about news and events related to them.

“It's impossible to coordinate us in order to change or ask for something. If your delegate is that kind of person who doesn't do anything it's almost impossible to fight for anything

  1. Miguel Gutiérrez, 26
  2. Complutense University of Madrid
